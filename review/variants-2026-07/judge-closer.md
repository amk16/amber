## Verdict (ranked, 3 sentences max each)
1. **artifact** — It is the only variant whose hook literally shows the product ("Here is the thing you're buying" + the FIG. 01 workflow-unit blueprint), so the buyer feels what they're purchasing before reading a word of prose. Its case cards lead with the outcome as a headline ("Associates shape drafts now, instead of assembling them"; "A decision-ready brief every week, with no research staff") then back it with narrative and capability chips — the most persuasive card structure of the three — and the electric-blue pill CTA is the highest-contrast, best-repeated ask without nagging. The "02 · What it does" benefit ladder (five lines that escalate with a scroll-highlight) is the strongest scroll-earning device on any of the pages.
2. **instrument** — Leads with the pain/benefit directly ("Systems that do the work your team retypes"), which converts harder than a conceptual opener, and the amber-on-black CTA plus the header scroll-% meter give both prominence and a felt sense of arc progression. Its "Recorded Work" cards (CLIENT/CASELOAD/BUILD + CONDITION/SYSTEM) are credible and detailed but read like a spec sheet — no outcome headline to hook the skim. Loses to artifact only on card framing and the missing product-visualization beat.
3. **ledger** — The most beautiful and internally coherent (the "typeset like an assessment" conceit is a genuine idea and the beats are cleanly labeled A/01→C/01), but for pure conversion the hook "Your assessment has already begun" is abstract — it presumes the offer rather than selling it. The oxblood CTAs are elegant but the quietest of the three, and BEFORE/BUILT/NOW cards are clear yet mechanical; it wins on craft, edges out on velocity.

## Graftable strengths (named element + which variant, one line each)
- **Workflow-unit blueprint (FIG. 01)** — artifact: a literal "here is what you're buying" diagram that de-risks the intangible before any copy.
- **Outcome-first case-card headline** — artifact: each proof card opens with the result, so the story is optional, not required.
- **Benefit-ladder with scroll-highlight ("02 · What it does")** — artifact: five escalating one-liners that pull the reader down the page.
- **Scroll-progress % meter in the header** — instrument: quietly signals arc position and manufactures momentum toward the ask.
- **Pain-forward hero headline ("...work your team retypes")** — instrument: names the buyer's pain in the first line instead of a concept.
- **Amber/blue high-contrast pill CTA** — instrument/artifact: an accent that makes the one ask unmissable against a restrained field.
- **"The first step costs twenty minutes and nothing else"** — artifact: reframes the ask as near-zero cost, the best de-risking close line.
- **"Assessment — written, yours to keep either way" as a repeated micro-line** — ledger: a low-commitment take-away that lowers the bar to booking.
