# PRODUCT.md — Archon Systems studio site

register: brand

## Product purpose

One-page conversion site for **Archon Systems**, Ali Malik's AI-systems studio. The studio builds AI agent systems for small owner-led firms (intake, drafting, reporting, operations intelligence), flat-fee. The site's single job: convince a firm owner that 20 minutes with this studio is worth it, and get them to the calendar link. Strategy source: `~/Desktop/docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-08-ai-systems-studio-design.md`; copy source: `~/Desktop/client-machine/site-copy.md`.

## Users

Owners and managing partners of 5–50-person firms: law firms, clinics, accounting practices, agencies. Mostly non-technical, 35–65, reading on a laptop between meetings or on a phone from a referral link. They are evaluating trustworthiness and competence, not features. Secondary audience: technical founders who will inspect the craft.

## Brand & tone

The diagnosing engineer: precise, calm, honest, zero hype. The site should feel like a beautifully typeset technical assessment — the deliverable the studio actually sells. Fixed prices said plainly. Understatement over superlatives.

**Honesty is a hard rule:** no invented metrics, no fake client counts, no fabricated telemetry. Two real case studies only (Chennai legal build; AI Marketing Pulse — the latter needs Kajol's sign-off before production deploy).

## Anti-references

- Crypto/AI-hype landing pages: neon glows, gradient text, "supercharge with AI"
- SaaS-cream template sites: cream + indigo blob illustrations, identical feature-card grids
- Fake-scale signals: logo walls, invented stats, "trusted by 500+ companies"
- Corporate-consultancy sites: stock photos of handshakes, "synergy" copy

## Bar

Reference class: the best small independent dev-studio sites (instrument.com-tier craft at one-page scale). "Excellent" = a technical founder would screenshot it; a firm owner would trust it.

## Strategic principles

1. Calendar CTA is the only conversion goal; it appears in nav, hero, and closing section.
2. Every claim on the page must be true today. The site earns trust it cannot afford to lose.
3.
